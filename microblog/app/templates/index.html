About HAR and DVS

Human activity recognition, or HAR, is a challenging time series classification task. It involves predicting the movement of a person based on sensor data and traditionally involves deep domain expertise and methods from signal processing to correctly engineer features from the raw data in order to fit a machine learning model.

The Dynamic Vision Sensor uses patented technology that works like the human retina. Only local pixel-level changes are transmitted at the time they occur. The result is a stream of events at microsecond time resolution, equivalent to conventional vision sensors running at thousands of frames per second â€“ but with far less data. Power, data storage and computational requirements are also drastically reduced, and sensor dynamic range is increased by orders of magnitude due to the local processing.
